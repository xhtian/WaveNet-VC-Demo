Cross-lingual Voice Conversion

Voice conversion (VC) modifies the speech of one speaker (source) to make it sound as if it is spoken by another speaker (target). Cross-lingual VC is a special case where source and target speakers speak different languages.

Baseline Framework

Monolingual PPGs.

Average Modeling Approach with Bilingual PPGs

We proposed an average modeling approach[1] making use of bilingual PPGs to represent the speaker-independent features of speech signals from different languages in the same feature space. In particular, we form the bilingual PPG by stacking two monolingual PPG vectors into one. The PPGs are extracted from two different monolingual automatic speech recognition systems. We utilize acoustic and linguistic information from many speakers in both source and target languages to train the average model which models the relationship between bilingual PPGs concatenating with speaker-specific i-vectors and the acoustic features.

Speech Samples

The model is evaluated with VCC2018[2] and a Mandarin average average model database[3].
